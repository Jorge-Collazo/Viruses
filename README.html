Viruses

What are they?

A virus is a malicious software, code, or program that is created to altar the way a computer works. They are also meant to spread from one computer to another. They act like real life viruses since both need a “host”. Computer viruses need a file or document. They attach themselves to legitimate programs or documents and later start to carry out their purpose.

Whats causes them?

Viruses are created by people that are always trying to infect others computers. Viruses can be spread in a number of ways, such as email attachments, downloaded files, or visits to unsecured websites.

Why are they created?

Viruses can be created to cause damage, or cause chaos. They might also be created for monetary or information gain. Some also believe that they might be created for bragging rights.

Interesting facts.

1. Viruses may hide in email attachments, even if it's an email from someone you know.

2. Viruses don't require user interaction, they simply need to execute their code.

3. Antivirus programs will not stop all threats.

4. In the past, viruses could be shared through floppy disks.

How it affects coding/online culture.

Viruses affect the way we may interact online, we are more careful about opening links and other attachments since some attachments contain viruses. You have probably known someone who was hacked after they opened a weird message or link. Because of viruses, there are also jobs and people dedicated to creating antivirus software.
